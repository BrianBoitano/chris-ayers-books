There is an empty case with your measurements, and it is waiting. The Gallery opens in the cold street outside a great museum of the dead, a hall of glass cases under a dome where every soul who could not bear to be forgotten stands behind glass with a brass label and a number, kept perfect, admired, witnessed, never let go. You came to find the likeness of who you were before you began to disappear. All you have to do is hold a pose, take your place, and let yourself be kept.

The Gallery is Book Five of ENCORE, a choose-your-path horror. The same Curator keeps the hall; the same company of the dead is recast into a new production, this one still, watched, and kept. Bring the character you built in The Haunt, The Vigil, The Understudy, and The Crossing, and the Gallery remembers what you carried, who you are, and what you have already proven you will and will not do.

You read by deciding. Sign the visitors' book or keep your hands in your pockets. Hold the look they want or stay a visitor passing through. Your choices decide which of the book's endings the glass gives you, and the way out, the true one, asks whether you can let yourself be forgotten completely, no likeness and no label and no eye to hold you, and walk out into the cold street as no one at all, real and alive.

If you like Fighting Fantasy or Choose Your Own Adventure, if you like interactive horror, if you like the cold anthology dread of American Horror Story, this is built for you.
